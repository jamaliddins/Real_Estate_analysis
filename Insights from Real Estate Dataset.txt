Insights from Real Estate Dataset

1. Testing the first hypothesis: Do Rental listings have a more right-skewed price distribution than sell listings, driven by luxury furnished apartments pulling the mean up?

The answer: No. Because both the rental skew p-value and the sell skew p-value are less than 0.05. Likewise, their skewness figures show almost the same distribution, just different SAR (Sales to Active Ratio) values.

2. Testing the second hypothesis: Do Price distributions in Riyadh and Jeddah are significantly wider (higher IQR) than in smaller cities, reflecting more market segmentation?

The answer: Yes. The spread has become significantly wider in major cities. To prove this hypothesis, Levene’s test was used, and it returned a positive stats value with p < 0.05. It can be seen easily by the IQR (Interquartile Range) difference: in Major cities, it is twice as high as in Small cities.
